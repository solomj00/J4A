About Me

Arlington is more than just a zip code to me. It is the town that raised me, the community where I chose to build my life, and the place where I am now raising my own children.

I grew up in Arlington, walking these same streets and attending our public schools. My connection to this town is not just sentimental; it is practical and deeply rooted. Arlington shaped who I am today.

Today, I'm raising my children in Arlington. For two years, I led the Dallin PTO, focusing on understanding the needs of new families and rebuilding our sense of community connection. I've coached youth sports, participated in school events, and invested deeply in the same institutions that served me as a child.

I see firsthand the challenges facing young families—from school facility needs to safe routes for our kids to bike and walk. These aren't abstract policy questions; they're daily realities.

When my parents needed support, I didn't send them away—I built an accessory dwelling unit to provide safe, single-level living for my mother. When my mother-in-law wanted to be closer to her grandchildren, I purchased a small condo in a multi-family building so she could age in place near family.

Multi-generational living has enriched my family's life immeasurably. These personal experiences taught me about the housing challenges facing seniors, the importance of accessibility, and the need for zoning laws that allow families to stay together.

I have navigated Arlington as a student in our public schools, as a homeowner, as a parent leading the Dallin PTO, and as a son helping my family age in place. This is the lifecycle of Arlington—and I understand what each generation needs to thrive.

Our Schools

Our schools have always been a point of pride. They served me well as a student, and they are serving my children well today.

Investing in Excellence

That reputation is built on sustained investment, and I am committed to keeping our school system performing at the highest tier. A strong school system requires more than just a budget; it requires community.

My Commitment:

  • Maintain Top-Tier Performance: Continue funding that supports academic excellence while maintaining fiscal responsibility
  • Upgrade School Facilities: Ensure our buildings meet 21st-century learning needs and provide safe, modern environments for students and educators
  • Support the Whole Student: Maintain programs, resources, and facilities that develop well-rounded individuals
  • Empower Educators: Ensure our teachers have the resources and support they need to succeed
  • Build Community: Drawing on my experience leading the Dallin PTO, foster stronger connections between families, schools, and the broader Arlington community

Quality education requires sustained commitment. As a parent and former PTO leader, I understand both the challenges facing our schools and the aspirations families have for their children.

Our Infrastructure

A livable town is a safe town. Whether you're driving to work, walking to the bus, or riding a bike to school, you deserve to get there safely.

Vision Zero: Designing for Safety

I have been deeply involved in examining our infrastructure through my work on the Park Ave Safety Study. I advocate for a Vision Zero approach where we actively design our streets to reduce accident rates to zero.

Safe Streets for All:

  • Complete the Minuteman Bikeway Connections: Create a comprehensive network of safe cycling routes connecting neighborhoods to schools, businesses, and recreational areas
  • Implement Smart Proactive Maintenance: Fix sidewalks and roads before they become hazards, not after. This approach saves money and prevents injuries
  • Support Complete Streets: Design infrastructure that works safely for pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers of all ages and abilities
  • Prioritize Pedestrian Safety: Enhance crosswalks, improve lighting, and create traffic calming measures in residential neighborhoods

Proactive Maintenance Saves Money

We cannot wait for our infrastructure to fail before we fix it. My experience on the Capital Planning Committee has shown me the financial logic of preventive maintenance.

The Math is Simple: When we fix a road today for $500,000, we avoid a $2 million reconstruction tomorrow. When we maintain sidewalks proactively, we prevent costly liability claims and keep our neighbors safe. Smart planning protects both our wallets and our community.

Infrastructure Investment Strategy:

  • Capital Planning Approach: Use data-driven assessment to prioritize repairs based on condition and projected lifespan
  • Road Maintenance Roadmap: Develop a multi-year plan that addresses our backlog systematically
  • Sidewalk Repair Program: Create a proactive schedule to fix deteriorating sidewalks before they become trip hazards
  • Bikeway Enhancement: Repair and improve the Minuteman Bikeway to maintain this valuable community asset

Our Future

Fiscal Responsibility and Sustainable Growth

Our town faces a challenging fiscal landscape. We rely heavily on residential property taxes to fund our services, placing a significant burden on homeowners. My time on the Capital Planning Committee has given me a clear view of our financial reality: we cannot rely solely on overrides to solve our structural deficits.

Smart Commercial Growth Strategy:

I support strategic commercial development along our major corridors like Massachusetts Avenue and Broadway. This is not just about bringing new businesses and amenities to our neighborhoods; it is about diversifying our tax base.

  • Diversify Revenue Sources: Increase the commercial share of our tax base to reduce pressure on residential property owners
  • Incentivize Quality Development: Attract businesses that enhance our walkable neighborhoods while generating sustainable revenue
  • Protect Neighborhood Character: Focus commercial growth on appropriate corridors while preserving residential areas
  • Fund Essential Services: Ensure we have resources to maintain roads, parks, schools, and public services without constantly asking residents for more

By increasing commercial tax revenue, we can protect homeowners and ensure we have the funds to maintain our infrastructure without constantly relying on overrides. This is fiscally sustainable growth.

Housing for Every Stage of Life

I believe Arlington should be a home for every stage of life. To make that a reality, our housing options must evolve. I have lived this value personally.

Creating Housing Options:

  • Support Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs): Allow homeowners to create in-law suites and small secondary units that enable multi-generational living
  • Enable Aging in Place: Ensure seniors can downsize without leaving the community they love
  • Provide Entry Points for Young Families: Create diverse housing stock that gives families a foothold in Arlington
  • Modernize Zoning for Inclusivity: Update regulations to reflect how families actually want to live—together, across generations

Multi-generational living has enriched my family's life. When I built an ADU for my mother and purchased a condo for my mother-in-law, I saw firsthand how the right housing options strengthen families and communities. I want to ensure our zoning laws allow others that same opportunity.
