Hi, I'm Joe and I'm running for Select Board!

Arlington is more than just a zip code to me. It is the town that raised me, the community where I chose to build my life, and the place where I am now raising my own children. My connection to this town is not just sentimental; it is practical and deeply rooted.

I've navigated Arlington as a student in our public schools, as a homeowner, as a parent leading the Dallin PTO, as a son helping my family age in place, and as a member of town government.

I am running for office because I want to ensure that Arlington remains a place of opportunity for every generation—from the students entering kindergarten to the seniors who have spent a lifetime building this community.

About Joe

A lifelong Arlington resident committed to community service

I grew up in Arlington, attended our public schools, and have spent my entire professional life working to make our community stronger. After graduating from Arlington High School, I went on to earn my degree in public policy and returned home to give back to the town that gave me so much.

As a parent of two young children in Arlington's school system, I understand firsthand the challenges and opportunities facing our families. I've served on the Dallin PTO, worked with local nonprofits, and currently serve on the Capital Planning Committee where I help make critical decisions about Arlington's infrastructure and long-term investments.

My professional background in municipal finance and urban planning has given me the tools to tackle complex budget challenges while maintaining the services our community depends on. I believe in data-driven decision making, transparent governance, and always putting residents first.

My Values

The principles guiding every decision I make for Arlington

Fiscal Responsibility

  • Belief in smart spending and long-term planning through Capital Planning experience
  • Focus on proactive maintenance over costly emergency repairs
  • Sustainable revenue through commercial growth to protect homeowners from tax burdens
  • Transparent budgeting that prioritizes core services and infrastructure

Community for All Generations

  • Arlington must work for students, families, and seniors alike
  • Supporting multi-generational living and housing diversity
  • Enabling seniors to age in place with dignity and accessibility
  • Investing in schools and youth programs for the next generation

Sustainable Development

  • Balanced growth that preserves Arlington's character while meeting modern needs
  • Environmental stewardship and climate action planning
  • Smart zoning that encourages affordable housing without overwhelming infrastructure
  • Protection of green spaces and community resources
