Born and Raised in Arlington

I grew up in Arlington, walking these same streets and attending our public schools. My connection to this town is not just sentimental; it is practical and deeply rooted. Arlington shaped who I am today.

Raising My Own Family

Today, I'm raising my children in Arlington. For two years, I led the Dallin PTO, focusing on understanding the needs of new families and rebuilding our sense of community connection. I've coached youth sports, participated in school events, and invested deeply in the same institutions that served me as a child.

I see firsthand the challenges facing young families‚Äîfrom school facility needs to safe routes for our kids to bike and walk. These aren't abstract policy questions; they're daily realities.

Caring for Family at Every Stage

When my parents needed support, I didn't send them away‚ÄîI built an accessory dwelling unit to provide safe, single-level living for my mother. When my mother-in-law wanted to be closer to her grandchildren, I purchased a small condo in a multi-family building so she could age in place near family.

Multi-generational living has enriched my family's life immeasurably. These personal experiences taught me about the housing challenges facing seniors, the importance of accessibility, and the need for zoning laws that allow families to stay together.

A Plan for Every Stage of Life

Policy grounded in real experience and fiscal responsibility.

üìö Excellence in Education

Our schools have always been a point of pride. They served me well as a student, and they are serving my children well today. That reputation is built on sustained investment, and I am committed to keeping our school system performing at the highest tier.

  • Maintain Top-Tier Performance: Continue funding that supports academic excellence while maintaining fiscal responsibility
  • Upgrade School Facilities: Ensure our buildings meet 21st-century learning needs
  • Support the Whole Student: Maintain programs and resources that develop well-rounded individuals
  • Empower Educators: Ensure teachers have the resources they need to succeed
  • Build Community: Foster stronger connections between families and schools

üö¥ Safe, Accessible Infrastructure

A livable town is a safe town. Through my work on the Park Ave Safety Study, I advocate for a Vision Zero approach where we actively design our streets to reduce accident rates to zero.

  • Complete the Minuteman Bikeway: Create comprehensive safe cycling routes
  • Smart Proactive Maintenance: Fix sidewalks and roads before they become hazards
  • Complete Streets Design: Infrastructure that works for pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers
  • Prioritize Pedestrian Safety: Enhanced crosswalks, lighting, and traffic calming

The Math is Simple: When we fix a road today for $500,000, we avoid a $2 million reconstruction tomorrow. Smart planning protects both our wallets and our community.
