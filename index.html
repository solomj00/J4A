Hi, I'm Joe and I'm running for Select Board!

Arlington is my home. It shaped who I am, and it is where I am raising my family today. I have experienced this town from many angles, as a student, a parent, a son of senior parents, and a member of town government.

I am running for office to make sure Arlington continues to work for everyone. For families just starting out, for the youth finding their path, and for folks who built this community and deserve to stay in it. With thoughtful planning and decisive leadership, we can enhance what makes Arlington special and prepare it for the future we owe the next generation.

About Me

I grew up in Arlington, attended our public schools, and after pursuing my education, returned home to give back to the town that gave me so much.

  • Town Meeting Member, Precinct 16
  • Vice Chair, Capital Planning Committee
  • PTO President, Dallin Elementary (2022-2024)

My professional background includes work at public agencies and private companies with a focus on producing results that people can see, measure, and trust. This experience gives me a unique ability to partner with other Select Board members and the Town Manager to prepare for our future. I believe in data-driven decision making, transparent governance, and always putting residents first.

My Values

The principles guiding every decision I make for Arlington

Sustainable Growth & Fiscal Responsibility

Smart commercial growth protects homeowners and funds the services we value. I support strategic development along Massachusetts Avenue and Broadway to diversify our tax base. By increasing commercial revenue, we reduce the burden on residential property taxes while ensuring we have the resources to maintain our roads, parks, and schools without constantly asking residents for more.

Excellence in Education

Our schools are Arlington's foundation. They served me well as a student and are serving my children well today. That reputation requires sustained investment and community support. As PTO President at Dallin, I focused on understanding new families' needs and rebuilding our sense of connection. I'm committed to supporting the whole student by maintaining our facilities and ensuring educators have the resources they need to succeed.

Safe Streets & Strong Infrastructure

Everyone deserves to get where they're going safely, whether driving, walking, or biking. Through my work on the Park Ave Safety Study, I advocate for a Vision Zero approach that actively designs streets to reduce accidents. I support proactive maintenance schedules for roads and sidewalks, and a clear roadmap for repairing and enhancing the Minuteman Bikeway. Smart maintenance now saves money later and keeps our town accessible for residents of all abilities.

Housing for Every Generation

Arlington should be home for every stage of life. I've lived this value personally—building an accessory dwelling unit for my mother's safe, single-level living, and purchasing a condo in an older multi-family building so my mother-in-law could be closer to her grandchildren. Multi-generational living has enriched my family, and I want our zoning laws to give others that same opportunity. These options allow seniors to downsize without leaving the community they love and give young families a foothold in our town.
